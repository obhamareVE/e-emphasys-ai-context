See also the corresponding Unicode listing of the ASCII character set. There also the used two- and three-letter acronyms are explained.

Note
  • Most of the ASCII characters are easily entered with a normal keyboard. For the first 32 characters (C0 Controls) most keyboards allow a combination of the Control-key plus the key corresponding to the ASCII character at an offset of 64 positions. For example, the key combination Ctrl-@ generates the NUL character, Ctrl-A generates SOH, etcetera.
  • The other way around, the first 32 characters (C0 Controls) are sometimes displayed as a Circumflex Accent ^ followed by the ASCII character at an offset of 64 positions. For example, the NUL character is displayed as ^@, the SOH character as ^A, etcetera. Likewise, the DEL character (position 127) is sometimes displayed as ^?.
