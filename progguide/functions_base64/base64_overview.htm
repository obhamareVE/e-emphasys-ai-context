Base64 overview

The Base64 Content-Transfer-Encoding standard is meant to encode binary data by means of simple printable characters. The used characters are 26 upper case letters (ABCDEFGHIJKLMNOPQRSTUVW), 26 lower case letters (abcdefghijklmnopqrstuvwxyz), 10 digits (0123456789), the plus character (+), and the forward slash (/), together 64 characters. For padding purposes, a 65th character is used: the equals sign (=). See Base64 Content-Transfer-Encoding for a complete description of the standard.
